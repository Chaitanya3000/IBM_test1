Malnutrition

Malnutrition is a health condition resulting from an insufficient or imbalanced intake of essential nutrients. It can lead to various health problems and hinder proper growth and development. Malnutrition can be caused by factors such as poor dietary choices, limited access to nutritious foods, inadequate healthcare, and socioeconomic challenges. It encompasses both undernutrition, where there's a deficiency of vital nutrients, and overnutrition, which involves excessive calorie intake without adequate nutrition. Malnutrition has far-reaching consequences on physical health, cognitive function, and immune response, impacting individuals and communities worldwide. Efforts to address malnutrition involve strategies to improve nutrition education, increase access to healthy foods, and enhance healthcare systems to ensure better overall well-being.
